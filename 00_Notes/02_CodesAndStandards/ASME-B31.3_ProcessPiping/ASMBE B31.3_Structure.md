# ASME B31.3 Process Piping: Summary and Reference Standards

## 1. Overview and Scope
The ASME B31.3 Process Piping code prescribes requirements for the materials, design, fabrication, assembly, erection, examination, inspection, and testing of piping. It is the primary code used for process piping systems typically found in petroleum refineries, chemical, pharmaceutical, textile, paper, semiconductor, and cryogenic plants, as well as related processing plants and terminals. Unlike B31.1 (which focuses on power plants and steam), B31.3 is tailored to handle a massive variety of fluid services, including highly toxic, flammable, and cryogenic fluids.

---

## 2. Summary by Chapter

### Chapter I: Scope and Definitions
* **Coverage:** Defines the limits of the code and outlines the different **Fluid Service Categories** which dictate the strictness of the rules applied (Normal, Category D, Category M, High Pressure, and High Purity).
* **Definitions:** Establishes precise terminology for process piping, such as defining the owner's responsibilities, inspectors, and stress intensification factors.

### Chapter II: Design
* **Design Conditions:** Details design pressure, design temperature, and allowances for pressure and temperature variations.
* **Pressure Design:** Provides equations for calculating minimum wall thickness for straight pipe, bends, and branch connections under internal and external pressure.
* **Flexibility and Support:** Addresses thermal expansion, stress analysis (sustained, occasional, and displacement loads), and the design of pipe supports to prevent failure from fatigue or overstress.

### Chapter III: Materials
* **Material Selection:** Outlines acceptable listed and unlisted materials for process environments.
* **Limitations and Toughness:** Heavily emphasizes temperature limitations. It includes stringent rules for impact testing to prevent brittle fracture in low-temperature and cryogenic fluid services.

### Chapter IV: Standards for Piping Components
* **Dimensional Standards:** Provides a mandatory table of accepted standards for valves, flanges, fittings, and gaskets. Components complying with these standards are considered acceptable for use under B31.3 without further pressure design calculations.

### Chapter V: Fabrication, Assembly, and Erection
* **Welding and Joining:** Details procedural requirements for welding, brazing, and soldering. 
* **Thermal Treatments:** Specifies strict requirements for preheating and Post-Weld Heat Treatment (PWHT) based on material thickness and alloy type to relieve residual stresses.
* **Assembly:** Covers the proper alignment, flanged joint assembly, and bolting procedures.

### Chapter VI: Inspection, Examination, and Testing
* **Examination:** Specifies the minimum required percentages for Nondestructive Examination (NDE)—such as visual, radiographic (RT), and ultrasonic (UT)—based on the fluid service category.
* **Testing:** Outlines the requirements for leak tightness testing, detailing the procedures for hydrostatic tests, pneumatic tests, and initial service leak tests.

### Chapter VII: Nonmetallic Piping and Piping Lined With Nonmetals
* **Specialized Materials:** Provides the specific rules for designing, bonding, and inspecting nonmetallic systems (like PVC, FRP, or CPVC) and metallic piping lined with nonmetals (like PTFE or glass-lined pipes), focusing on their unique flexibility and pressure limitations.

### Chapter VIII: Piping for Category M Fluid Service
* **Toxic Fluids:** Applies strictly to fluid services where a single exposure to a very small quantity of a toxic fluid can produce serious, irreversible harm. It demands enhanced design safety margins, stricter material limitations, and 100% radiographic examination of welds.

### Chapter IX: High Pressure Piping
* **Extreme Pressures:** Covers piping designed for pressures typically in excess of ASME B16.5 Class 2500 ratings. It completely replaces the design formulas of Chapter II with heavy-wall equations and mandates extensive fatigue analysis and impact testing for all materials.

### Chapter X: High Purity Piping
* **Clean Applications:** Addresses piping systems requiring specialized cleaning and fabrication methods to prevent contamination of the fluid. It is heavily utilized in the semiconductor, pharmaceutical, and bioprocessing industries.

## 3. Other Standards Mentioned and Categorized

Because B31.3 covers a vast array of chemicals and environments, it references numerous external codes for components and material compatibility. Below is a categorized list.

### A. ASME Boiler and Pressure Vessel Code (BPVC)
* **ASME BPVC Section VIII, Division 1 and 2 - Rules for Construction of Pressure Vessels:** Referenced for calculating specific stress values, flange design formulas, and unlisted component design.
* **ASME BPVC Section IX - Welding, Brazing, and Fusing Qualifications:** The definitive standard used by B31.3 for qualifying welders, brazers, and their corresponding procedures (WPS/PQR).

### B. ASME Component and Dimensional Standards
* **ASME B16.5 - Pipe Flanges and Flanged Fittings:** The primary standard for pressure-temperature ratings, materials, and dimensions of steel flanges.
* **ASME B16.9 - Factory-Made Wrought Buttwelding Fittings:** Covers overall dimensions, tolerances, and testing for elbows, tees, and reducers.
* **ASME B16.11 - Forged Fittings, Socket-Welding and Threaded:** Dimensional and material standards for smaller, high-pressure forged fittings.
* **ASME B16.34 - Valves - Flanged, Threaded, and Welding End:** Provides pressure-temperature ratings and minimum wall thickness requirements for process valves.
* **ASME B36.10M / B36.19M - Welded and Seamless Wrought Steel / Stainless Steel Pipe:** Standardizes the outside diameters and wall thicknesses (schedules) for piping.
* **ASME BPE - Bioprocessing Equipment:** Often used in conjunction with B31.3 Chapter X for high-purity pharmaceutical piping.

### C. API (American Petroleum Institute)
* **API 5L - Specification for Line Pipe:** Heavily used in refinery off-sites and process pipelines for seamless and welded steel pipe.
* **API 570 - Piping Inspection Code:** Used alongside B31.3; while B31.3 governs *new* construction, API 570 governs the in-service inspection, repair, and alteration of process piping.
* **API 600 / API 602 / API 608:** Valve design standards specific to the oil and gas process industry (Gate valves, compact steel valves, ball valves).

### D. ASTM (American Society for Testing and Materials)
* **ASTM A53 / A106:** Specifications for seamless and welded carbon steel pipe (A106 is preferred for higher temperature process services).
* **ASTM A312:** Standard specification for seamless, welded, and heavily cold worked austenitic stainless steel pipes (e.g., 304/316).
* **ASTM A333:** Standard specification for seamless and welded steel pipe for low-temperature service (crucial for B31.3 cryogenic services).

### E. NACE International / ISO
* **NACE MR0175 / ISO 15156 - Materials for use in H2S-containing environments in oil and gas production:** Extremely important in B31.3 for "sour service" piping to prevent sulfide stress cracking (SSC).
* **NACE MR0103 - Materials Resistant to Sulfide Stress Cracking in Corrosive Petroleum Refining Environments:** Similar to MR0175 but specifically tailored for downstream refinery process piping.

### F. MSS (Manufacturers Standardization Society)
* **MSS SP-58 - Pipe Hangers and Supports:** Governs materials, design, manufacture, and inspection of pipe supports.
* **MSS SP-97 - Integrally Reinforced Forged Branch Outlet Fittings:** Governs O-lets (Weldolets, Sockolets) heavily used for B31.3 branch connections.

# ASME B31.1-2022 Power Piping: Summary and Reference Standards

## 1. Overview and Scope
The ASME B31.1-2022 Power Piping code prescribes the minimum requirements for the design, materials, fabrication, erection, examination, testing, inspection, operation, and maintenance of piping systems. It primarily applies to electric power generating stations, industrial and institutional plants, geothermal heating systems, and central and district heating and cooling systems.

---

## 2. Summary by Chapter

### Chapter I: Scope and Definitions
* **Coverage:** Defines the jurisdictional limits of the code, detailing which types of boiler external piping and nonboiler external piping are included.
* **Exclusions:** Excludes certain components like towers, building frames, tanks, and marine installations.
* **Definitions:** Establishes precise terminology used throughout the standard, such as definitions for the owner, packaged equipment, pipe versus tube, and weldment.

### Chapter II: Design
* **Conditions and Criteria:** Details design pressures, temperatures, ambient influences, and criteria for determining proper ratings.
* **Pressure Design:** Provides equations and guidelines for calculating the minimum required wall thickness of straight pipes, bends, branch connections, and flanges.
* **Selection and Limitations:** Guides the appropriate selection of piping components and joints, including welded, flanged, and threaded types. 
* **Flexibility:** Addresses expansion, flexibility analysis, and pipe-supporting elements.

### Chapter III: Materials
* **Material Selection:** Outlines the rules for selecting listed materials and establishes guidelines for requesting the adoption of new or unlisted materials.
* **Limitations:** Imposes restrictions based on tensile and yield strengths, temperature limits, and provides specific requirements for defect repairs on steel castings.

### Chapter IV: Reference Specifications, Codes, and Standards
* **Compliance:** Lists the mandatory standards and specifications that standard and nonstandard piping components must adhere to for Code compliance.

### Chapter V: Fabrication, Assembly, and Erection
* **Joining Processes:** Details extensive procedural requirements for welding, brazing, and soldering, including the qualification of procedures and operators.
* **Fabrication Rules:** Provides standards for bending, forming, pipe support attachment, preheating, and postweld heat treatment.

### Chapter VI: Inspection, Examination, and Testing
* **Inspection:** Defines the responsibilities of the inspector prior to and during operation to verify compliance.
* **Examination:** Specifies requirements for nondestructive examinations (NDEs), including visual and radiographic methods.
* **Testing:** Outlines the parameters for pressure tests, such as hydrostatic and pneumatic testing, to ensure system leak tightness.

### Chapter VII: Operation and Maintenance
* **System Lifecycle:** Focuses on maintaining systems post-construction, emphasizing condition assessment programs for Covered Piping Systems (CPS).

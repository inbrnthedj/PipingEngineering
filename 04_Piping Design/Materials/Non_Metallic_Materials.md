# Non-Metallic Piping Materials

Non-metallic materials are highly preferred in utility networks, wastewater treatment, and chemical distribution because they completely eliminate the risk of galvanic corrosion and chemical rust.

## 1. Thermoplastics (Pure Plastics)
Polymers that can be repeatedly melted and solidified. They have distinct temperature limits (typically under 60°C–90°C) and are joined via solvent cement welding or heat-fusion.
* **PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride):** Generic utility plastic. Ideal for atmospheric drainage, vents, and standard water transport.
* **CPVC (Chlorinated PVC):** Offers a higher temperature threshold than standard PVC. Used for hot water lines and harsher chemical blends.
* **PP (Polypropylene):** Highly resilient against aggressive industrial alkalis and strong acids.
* **HDPE (High-Density Polyethylene):** Tough and flexible. Often buried underground because it flexes without cracking under soil shifts. Ideal for long firewater loops.

## 2. Thermosetting Plastics (Fiberglass Composites)
Glass fibers embedded inside a chemically cured resin (like epoxy). Once cured, they cannot be melted down. They function like high-strength composite metallic alternatives.
* **GRP / GRE (Glass Reinforced Plastic/Epoxy):** Highly favored for large-diameter overhead piping headers. They handle heavy internal pressures and marine/seawater conditions without weight penalties. Joined using specialized chemical resin kit lamination.

## Material Selection by Plant Application

### A. Water Treatment Plants
Water treatment scales from acidic chemical treatment down to potable distribution, heavily leaning on plastics:
* **Chemical Dosing Lines (Chlorine, Acids):** **PVC, CPVC, or PP**. Highly corrosive chemicals would eat through standard steel walls.
* **Main Water Run Headers:** **HDPE** (underground firewater and potable feeds) and **GRP/GRE** (large diameter overhead treatment headers).

### B. Chiller Refurbishing Plants
Chiller plants use compression loops to pull process fluid temperatures down into sub-zero domains (e.g., -26°C).
* **The Temperature Constraint:** Standard thermoplastics like PVC become extremely brittle at sub-zero temperatures and crack under pressure.
* **Material Mapping:** * For sub-zero refrigeration loops, specialized metallics like **Low-Temperature Carbon Steel (LTCS, ASTM A333 Grade 6)** must be sourced.
  * Plastics (**HDPE or GRP**) are strictly kept on the ambient **Cooling Water Loop** running out to external cooling towers where water immunity is needed.

# Piping Materials Overview

In piping engineering and material take-offs (MTOs), piping systems are broadly categorized by their core compound chemistry into **Metallic** and **Non-Metallic** materials. Selecting the right category dictates the pressure-temperature ratings, joining methods, welding requirements, and cost tracking of the project.

## Master Classification Tree

1. [**Metallic Materials**](./Metallic_Materials.md)
   * **Carbon Steel (CS):** The benchmark industrial material for general strength.
   * **Stainless Steel (SS / AISI):** High-chromium alloys for rust and chemical prevention.
   * **Alloy Steel:** High-temperature/high-pressure specialty steels (e.g., Chrome-Moly).
2. [**Non-Metallic Materials**](Non_Metallic_Materials.md)
   * **Thermoplastics:** Pure polymers that soften with heat; joined by solvent glue or heat fusion.
   * **Thermosetting / Fiberglass:** Reinforced plastic composites; chemically cured and un-meltable.
   * **Lined Piping:** Metallic strength outer shell with a non-metallic inner chemical sleeve.

## Quick-Selection Matrix

| Operational Factor | Preferred Material Class | Common Plant Application |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| High Pressure + High Temp | Metallic (Carbon Steel / Alloys) | High-Pressure Steam lines, Hydrocarbons |
| High Chemical Corrosion | Non-Metallic (Thermoplastics) or SS | Chemical dosing loops, Acid/Alkali transport |
| Low Temperature Utility | Non-Metallic (HDPE) or Steel | Cooling water loops, Chiller utility lines |
| Marine / Seawater Loops | Non-Metallic (GRP/GRE) or Duplex SS | Seawater intake, Cooling tower water headers |

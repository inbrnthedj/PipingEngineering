# Metallic Piping Materials

Metallic materials handle the pressure-retaining limits of standard process plants. They are grouped by chemistry to address specific operational environments like corrosion, intense heat, or sub-zero cooling.

## 1. Carbon Steel (CS)
* **Characteristics:** Composed primarily of Iron and Carbon ($<0.30\%$). Highly susceptible to rust if exposed to water and oxygen, but provides excellent structural strength and economy.
* **Primary Use:** Ambient or high-temperature non-corrosive fluids (utility air, standard hydrocarbons, steam, process water).

## 2. Stainless Steel (SS)
* **Characteristics:** Contains a minimum of $10.5\%$ Chromium, creating a passive oxide layer that prevents oxidation (rusting).
* **Subcategories in MTO Work:**
  * **AISI Grades (Austenitic):** Standard 300-series grades. **AISI 304** is standard; **AISI 316** adds Molybdenum for superior resistance against chemical pitting (chlorides/acids). Note: In MTO codes, look for suffixes like "L" (e.g., 316L) representing Low Carbon, which prevents cracking during welding.
  * **Duplex Stainless Steel:** A hybrid micro-structure providing double the physical strength of standard AISI grades alongside extreme corrosion resistance. Used heavily in high-salinity seawater applications.

## 3. Alloy Steel (Chrome-Moly)
* **Characteristics:** Low-alloy steels engineered with specific ratios of Chromium and Molybdenum. 
* **Primary Use:** Extreme high-temperature services where standard carbon steel would soften and suffer from mechanical "creep" over time.
* **Common Subcategory:** **P11 / F11** (1.25% Chrome - 0.5% Moly), which is highly common in power plants and refinery steam distribution networks.

## Master Metallic Differentiation Table

| Material Class | Common MTO Short-hand Code | Matching Material Properties | Typical Process Service |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Carbon Steel** | CS (e.g., A106-B) | Economical, strong, rusts | Instrument Air, Plant Steam, Non-corrosive Hydrocarbons |
| **Stainless Steel** | AISI 304 / 316 / 316L | Rustproof, acid-resistant | Corrosive Chemicals, Food/Purity lines, Demineralized Water |
| **Duplex SS** | Duplex (e.g., UNS S31803) | Extremely high strength + salt proof | Offshore Oil & Gas, Coastal Seawater intake loops |
| **Alloy Steel** | P11 / P22 / P91 | Maintained strength at extreme heat | Superheated Power Piping, Refinery Cracking furnaces |

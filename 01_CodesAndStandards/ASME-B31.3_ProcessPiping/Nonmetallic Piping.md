# Nonmetallic Piping
*   ASME B31.3 provides a list of components for nonmetallic piping in **Table A326.1** (Before the 2024 edition) or **Table A326.1-1 (Nonmetallic and Nonmetallic-Lined Piping Component standards)** in the 2024 edition
*   Table A326.1-1 strictly tabulates specific listed codes from testing organizations like ASTM and API governing nonmetallic assemblies. It includes:
    - *Nonmetallic Fittings, Valves, and Flanges* (e.g., ASTM D2464 for threaded PVC fittings)
    - *Nonmetallic Pipes and Tubes* (e.g. API15LE for PE line pipe, ASTM D1785 for PVC pipe schedules, and ASTM D2996 for filament-wound fiberglass RTR pipe)
*   If a component or material standard does not appear in this table or Appendix B, it is structurally considered an **"Unlisted Component"** and triggers extra qualification burdens for the piping engineer

## Subcategories
ASME B31.3 divides nonmetallic piping into several core subcategories:
1. **Thermoplastics:** Plastics that can be repeatedly softened by heating and hardened by cooling
    - PVC, CPVC, Polyethylene (PE), Polypropylene (PP), PVDF, etc.
2. **Reinforced Thermosettling Resins (RTR):** Resins that change irreversibly into an infusible product when cured, typically reinforced with glass fibers (often broadly called fiberglass or glass-reinforced plastic)
3. **Reinforced Plastic Mortar (RPM):** Similar to RTR but incorporating an aggregate filler like sand.
4. **Brittle Materials:** Nonplastic components such as borosilicate process glass and porcelain.
5. **Lined Systems:** Standard metallic host pipes bounded internally with bonded or loose thick plastic linings 
    - PTFE or PVDF

## Differences from Metallic Piping
- **Stress-Strain Behavior:** Unlike metals, the stress-strain behavior of nonmetals varies drastically and is less easily predicted by simple linear elastic formulas. Thermoplastics exhibit plastic/creep-type deformations even under relatively minor localized strains.  
    - See **Appendix B** for allowable stress values for some thermoplastic and reinforced thermosetting resin pipe
- **Strict Design Limits:** Because of their material stability parameters, no allowances are permitted for variations in pressure or temperature above the baseline design conditions. Systems must always be engineered for the single most severe coincident pressure and temperature.  
    - See **Table B-4** for allowable pressures for reinforced concrete pipe
    - See **Table B-5** for allowable pressures for borosilicate glass pipe
- **Joining Limitations:** Rather than traditional metal welding, joining relies on specialized techniques like thermoplastic heat fusion, electrofusion, solvent cementing, adhesive bonding, or mechanical gasketing.  
- **Service Exclusions:** Chapter VII expressly makes no provision for nonmetallic lines to operate under severe cyclic conditions.

- Radiographic

- Hydrostatic *(preferred method for nonmetallic piping)*
- Pneumatic
- Hydrostatic-pneumatic
- Sensitive

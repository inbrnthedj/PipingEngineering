# High Pressure Piping
## Definition
A high pressure fluid service is a fluid service for which the owner specifies the use of Chapter IX for piping design and construction
* The owner is responsible for indicating which piping system is in High Pressure Service Fluid - see **ASME Section VIII, Division 3: Alternative Rules for Construction of High Pressure Vessels**

## Conditions and criterias
- Alternate wall thickness calculations and provisions are provided and are going to be thinner than the base code for the same pressure, but it will not be thin
- Additional material toughness, analysis during desing, inspection and testing are required
- No provision is made for Cat M Fluid Service

## Materials
- Appendix K, Table K-1: Allowable stress values
- Materials may not be used above the max temp for which allowable stresses are provided
- Nonmetallic parts, other than gaskets and packing, are not permitted.

## Fabrication
- ASME BPVC Section IX: Requirements for qualification of welding procedures and welders / welding operators
- General fabrication requirements, end preparation, alignment, welding, preheat, post-weld heat treatment

## Examination Types
- Visual
- Radiographic
- Ultrasonic

* In general, 100% examinatuion is required (materials and components)
    - girth, longitudinal, and branch connection welds by radiography or ultrasonic examination as an alternative
    - In-process examination is not an acceptable alternative
    - **Table K341.3.2:** Acceptance criteria for welds

## Testing Types
1. **Full-pressure test**
    - all components except for bolting and gaskets and pressure-relieving devices
    - can be done on piping subassemblies before erection, not necessarily during installation
2. **Leak Test**
    - in 2014, leak test pressure is changed from 1.5 times the design pressure corrected for temperature to **1.25 times the design pressure corrected for temperature**

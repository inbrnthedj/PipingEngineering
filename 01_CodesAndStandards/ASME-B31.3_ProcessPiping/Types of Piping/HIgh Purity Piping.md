# High Purity Piping

## Definition
High Purity Piping is a fluid service that requires alternative methods of fabrication, inspection, examination, and testing with the intent to produce a controlled level of cleanness
- applies to piping systems defined as high purity, ultra-high purity, hygienic or aseptic

* The owner is responsible for indicating which piping system is in High Purity Fluid Service. High Pressure Service Fluid is not covered under High PUrity Piping
* Additional requirements are provided for:
- uninsulated components
- tube fittings
- valves
- flanges
- blanks
- joints

## COnditions and Criteria
- **B31.3 Chapter X:** Rules for piping design and construction
- Design Pressure and Temperature

## Prohibited Components
- Expanded joint flanges
- flared tube fittings
- caulked joints

# Examination types
- Visual
- Weld Coupon
- Radiography
- Ultrasonic
- In-process

* For Cat. M Fluid Service, a 20% random radiography or ultrasonic examination is allowed. In-process examination can be used as an alternative.

## Testing Types
- Pneumatic leak test (preferred)
- Sensitive Leak Test
- Helium Mass Spectrometer (may be used in lieu of pneumatic test at the request of the owner)
- Alternative leak test

# FLUID SERVICE

## Category M 
- a fluid service in which the potential for personel exposure is judged to be significant.

A fluid service is said to be a Category M if ALL of the following apply:
1. It is highly toxic that a single exposure to a small quantity of fluid caused by leakage can produce serious irreversible harm to people on breathing or bodily contact, even when prompt restorative measures are taken;
2. If, even after consideration of piping design, experience, service condition and location, the owner determines that the requirements for Normal Fluid Service do not sufficiently provide **LEAK TIGHTNESS** required to protect personnel from exposure.

For a further guide, refer to Figure M300 of ASME B31.3-2020 (Guide to Classifying Fluid Services)

## Category D
A fluid service is said to be a Category D if ALL of the following apply:
1. The fluid is nonflammable, nontoxic, and not damaging to human tissues as defined in Par. 300.22;
2. The design gage pressure does not exceed 1035 kPa (150 psi);
3. The design temperature is not greater than 186°C (366°F)
4. The fluid temp caused by anything other than atmospheric conditions is not less than -29°C (-20°F)

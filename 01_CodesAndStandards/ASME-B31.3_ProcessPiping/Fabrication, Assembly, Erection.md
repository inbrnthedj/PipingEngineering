# Fabrication, Assembly, and Erection

ASME B31.3 Chapter V tackles welding requirements, types of welded joints, conditions for preheating, heat treatment, bending and forming, brazing and soldering, assembly, and erection.

### Fabrication Processes:

1. **Welding**
    - The employer/owner is responsible for the welding performed and qualifying of welding procedures and welders.
    - Welding procedures and welders shall be qualified as per ASME BPVC, Section IX.

2. **Preheating**
    - Is used to minimize detrimental effects of high temperature and severe thermal gradients in welding, prevent hydrogen cracking, and improve the metallurgical properties of the material.
    - Requirements for preheat are a function of:
        - The P-Number of the base material.
        - Nominal wall thickness.
        - Specified minimum tensile strength of the base metal.
    - Preheating requirements are discussed in ASME B31.3 Par. 330 and provided in Table 330.1.1:
        - For most Carbon Steel (CS) and Stainless Steel (SS), it requires preheat to 10°C (50°F).
        - For most low alloy steels, it requires preheat to 120°C (250°F).

3. **Heat Treatment**
    - Is primarily used to relieve residual stresses, improve ductility, and alter the metallurgical structure of the weldment after fabrication.
    - Specific requirements for post-weld heat treatment (PWHT) for welded materials are included in Table 331.1.1.
    - The designer has to specify more or less stringent heat treatment requirements:
        - See Par. 331 and Table 331.1.1 for requirements of heat treatment.
        - Heat treatment is required after certain welding, bending, and forming operations. See exceptions in Par. 331.2 and Table 331.1.3.
    - The code does not require heat treatment for CS if:
        - Multilayer welds are used when $T > 5\text{ mm}$, and
        - Preheat to 95°C (200°F) is used when $T > 25\text{ mm}$.

4. **Bending and Forming**
    - Pipes may be bent and components formed by any hot or cold method suitable for the material use, fluid service, and the bending or forming process (e.g., steam traced pipe / *tracciatura a vapore*).
    - Requirements for bend flattening and forming temperatures are provided by the code.
    - Heat treatment is required for certain materials after bending and forming.

5. **Brazing and Soldering**
    - Refer to ASME Section IX, Part QB for the qualification of brazing procedures, brazers, and brazing operators.
    - Refer to *The Copper Tube Handbook* by the Copper Development Association (CDA) for soldering procedures.
